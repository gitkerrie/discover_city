Classic food city · Sichuan

Chengdu

Red chili oil, sweet heat, and a city that turns every meal into a daily ritual.

Chengdu is about far more than heat. The numbing lift of Sichuan pepper, the aroma of chili oil, and sweet-savory sauces create layers of classic Sichuan flavor. Street snacks and neighborhood restaurants flow into one another, keeping the city delicious from breakfast to late night.

Numbing heat Street-snack culture All-day dining
01

What to eat in Chengdu

  1. 01

    Zhong Shui Jiao

    Thin-skinned pork dumplings dressed with sweet soy sauce and red chili oil.

  2. 02

    Dan Dan Noodles

    Fine noodles coated with preserved greens, minced pork, and fragrant chili oil.

  3. 03

    Long Chao Shou

    Delicate wontons served either in a clear broth or a vivid red-oil sauce.

  4. 04

    Mapo Tofu

    Tender tofu, minced beef, chile bean paste, and Sichuan pepper in a vivid numbing sauce.

02

Eat like a local

Begin with red-oil wontons in the morning, graze on snacks in the afternoon, and save dinner for a full Sichuan meal.
