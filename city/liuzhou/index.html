Hidden-gem food city · Guangxi

Liuzhou

Sour, hot, savory, and steaming flavors converge in one audacious bowl of noodles.

Liuzhou is famous for luosifen, but the deeper story is its culture of pickled flavor. Sour bamboo shoots, beans, wood ear, and chili oil build complexity, while rice noodles and braised snacks carry that bold profile through everyday meals.

What to eat in Liuzhou

  1. 01

    Luosifen

    A savory snail broth with sour bamboo shoots, crisp tofu skin, peanuts, and chili.

    Braised Duck Feet

    Fried duck feet soften in a rich braise with snails and sour bamboo shoots.

  3. 03

    Liuzhou Filtered Rice Noodles

    Fresh rice batter is filtered into tender noodles with pork and pickled beans.

  4. 04

    Savory Glutinous Rice Balls

    Soft rice dumplings enclose a seasoned pork filling.

Eat like a local

Taste the broth before adding chili, and add sour bamboo shoots gradually until the balance feels right.
